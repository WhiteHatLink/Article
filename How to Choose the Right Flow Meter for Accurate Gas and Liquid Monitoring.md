<p><strong>Understanding Flow Measurement Basics</strong></p>
<p>Flow measurement is the process of calculating how much fluid passes through a system over time. This fluid can be a liquid like water or oil, or a gas like air or natural gas.</p>
<p>There are two main types of flow measurement:</p>
<p><strong>Volumetric Flow</strong></p>
<p>This measures the volume of fluid, such as liters per minute.</p>
<p><strong>Mass Flow</strong></p>
<p>This measures the mass of fluid, such as kilograms per hour. Mass flow is more accurate because it is not affected by temperature or pressure changes.</p>
<p>Many industrial experts explain that &ldquo;mass flow measurement gives more stable results in changing conditions,&rdquo; which is why it is often preferred for gas systems.</p>
