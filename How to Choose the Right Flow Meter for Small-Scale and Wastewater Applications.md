<p><strong>Understanding Flow Measurement</strong></p>
<p>Flow measurement means calculating how much fluid passes through a pipe over time. The fluid can be clean water, chemicals, or wastewater.</p>
<p>There are two main types of measurement:</p>
<p><strong>Volumetric Flow</strong></p>
<p>Measures the volume of fluid, such as liters per minute.</p>
<p><strong>Mass Flow</strong></p>
<p>Measures the mass, such as kilograms per hour. This method is more stable because it is not affected by temperature or pressure.</p>
<p>Industry knowledge often explains that &ldquo;accurate flow measurement is essential for process control and efficiency,&rdquo; especially in systems where precision matters.</p>
<p><strong>Why Small-Scale and Wastewater Systems Need Special Flow Meters</strong></p>

<p><strong>Advanced Technologies Used in Flow Measurement</strong></p>
<p>Modern flow meters use advanced technologies to improve accuracy and performance.</p>
<p><strong>Electromagnetic Technology</strong></p>
<p>This technology is often used in wastewater systems. It measures conductive liquids and works well even with dirty fluids.</p>
<p><strong>Ultrasonic Technology</strong></p>
<p>Ultrasonic meters use sound waves to measure flow. They are non-invasive and easy to install.</p>
<p><strong>Thermal Technology</strong></p>
<p>Thermal flow meters are used for gases and low flow rates.</p>
